CPython parity: Python/codegen.c (3.14.0): module-level

The v0.6.12 release teaches the codegen package the next
module-level shape after the v0.6.11 init+aug-assign pair: a
single `<target> = <left> <op> <right>` statement where target,
left, and right are all Names of 1..15 ASCII chars and `<op>`
is any of the 13 BinOp operators the classifier supports
(arithmetic + bitwise + MatMult).

Bytecode pattern: RESUME, LOAD_NAME left, LOAD_NAME right,
BINARY_OP <oparg> (with 5 cache words), STORE_NAME target,
LOAD_CONST None, RETURN_VALUE. co_consts = [None]; co_names =
[left, right, target] (insertion order); co.StackSize = 2.

This is the first codegen path to drive `BINARY_OP` with the
non-inplace operator family â€” same inline-cache shape as
v0.6.11's `+=` but with names instead of integers on both
sides.

The 246/246 oracle stays byte-identical. The parallel-output
gate (`TestCodegenParity`) automatically lights up fixtures
089-100 (12 binop fixtures spanning every BINARY_OP operator
the classifier ships with name-only operands).

## Note on roadmap deviation

Spec 1508 originally allocated v0.6.12 to comprehensions and
generators. Adding those now means landing function scope,
frame-locals, MAKE_CELL, GET_ITER + FOR_ITER + JUMP loops,
LOAD_FAST/STORE_FAST, GET_AWAITABLE, RESUME variants for
generators â€” a step that needs codegen to first own the basic
expression and control-flow surface. modBinOpAssign is the
next natural step after aug-assign: same BINARY_OP family,
non-inplace branch, names on both sides.

After v0.6.12 codegen owns every name-only single-line BinOp
shape that exists in the classifier. Comprehensions /
generators slide to a later sub-release; slot 1522 retains its
allocation (driver/perf, classifier funeral).
