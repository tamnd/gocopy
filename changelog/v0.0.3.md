## v0.0.3

`gocopy compile` accepts multiple no-op statements on consecutive
lines. The N=1 case is v0.0.2; everything from `pass\npass\n` up
through five mixed constants on five lines now matches
`python3.14 -m py_compile` byte-for-byte.

What CPython does for an N-statement no-op body:

- bytecode: `RESUME` + (N-1) × `NOP` + `LOAD_CONST 0` + `RETURN_VALUE`
- consts tuple: still `(None,)`
- line table: synthetic prologue + (N-1) × `ONE_LINE1`(1 unit) +
  one `ONE_LINE1`(2 units) entry per real statement

We mirror that exactly.

### Added

- `bytecode.NoOpBytecode(n)` and `bytecode.LineTableNoOps(endCols)`
  generalise the v0.0.2 single-no-op helpers. The single-no-op
  helper is now a thin wrapper.
- `bytecode.NOP` opcode constant (CPython 3.14 opcode 27).
- Five new fixtures: `009_two_pass.py` through `013_five_consts.py`.

### Changed

- The classifier in `compiler` now collects a slice of statement
  end columns instead of returning a single one. Body shape
  enum collapses `modSingleNoOp` into a general `modNoOps`.

### Out of scope (deferred)

- Blank or comment lines BETWEEN statements. CPython encodes the
  larger line delta with `ONE_LINE0` / `ONE_LINE2` entries; the
  encoder upgrade lands in v0.0.4.
- String / bytes literal as a top-level statement (docstring path).
- gopapy as the parser (waits on a gopapy v1.0.0 cut; see v0.0.2
  notes).
